# 2016-2017 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2016 - June 30, 2017

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 155 | 57.4% |
| 4-5 years | 63 | 23.3% |
| 5-6 years | 17 | 6.3% |
| 6-7 years | 14 | 5.2% |
| 7+ years | 21 | 7.8% |

**Total Analyzed:** 270 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 155 graduates (57.4%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 63 graduates (23.3%)
- **5-6 years:** 17 graduates (6.3%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 80 graduates (29.6%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 14 graduates (5.2%)
- **7+ years:** 21 graduates (7.8%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 35 graduates (13.0%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Majority complete quickly:** 57.4% finish in under 4 years (likely transfer students)
- **Significant extended completion:** 29.6% take 4-6 years
- **Long-term persistence:** 13.0% take 6+ years to complete
