# 2023-2024 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2023 - June 30, 2024

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 40 | 51.9% |
| 4-5 years | 12 | 15.6% |
| 5-6 years | 7 | 9.1% |
| 6-7 years | 9 | 11.7% |
| 7+ years | 9 | 11.7% |

**Total Analyzed:** 77 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 40 graduates (51.9%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 12 graduates (15.6%)
- **5-6 years:** 7 graduates (9.1%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 19 graduates (24.7%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 9 graduates (11.7%)
- **7+ years:** 9 graduates (11.7%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 18 graduates (23.4%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Majority complete quickly:** 51.9% finish in under 4 years (likely transfer students)
- **Significant extended completion:** 24.7% take 4-6 years
- **Long-term persistence:** 23.4% take 6+ years to complete
