# Wiley College - Graduation and Retention Statistics

**Source:** https://www.collegefactual.com/colleges/wiley-college/academic-life/graduation-and-retention/

## Key Retention Rate

**Freshman Retention Rate:** 70%
- Similar to the national average of 70.57%

## Graduation Rates

**Four-Year Graduation Rate:** 21%

**Six-Year Graduation Rate:** 25% (official completion metric)

**Eight-Year Graduation Rate:** 31%

**Average Time to Degree:** 4.29 years

## Non-Completion Outcomes (8-Year Cohort)

**Total Students Who Did Not Complete:** 360

**Breakdown:**
- Still enrolled: 1 student (0.3%)
- Transferred: 85 students (23.6%)
- Dropped out: 253 students (70.3%)

## Graduation Rates by Demographic (6-Year)

**By Race/Ethnicity:**
- Black/African-American: 28%
- Hispanic: 33%
- International: 55%

## First-Time, Full-Time Students

**Cohort Size:** 61% of all students

**Graduation Rates:**
- 4-year rate: 21%
- 6-year rate: 33%
- 8-year rate: 33%

## National Comparison

**Wiley's Performance vs. National Average:**
- Wiley College 6-year rate: 25%
- National average (similar institutions): 46.43%
- **Gap:** -21.43 percentage points

**Key Finding:** Wiley's 25% six-year graduation rate significantly trails the national average for similar full-time, first-time students.

## Critical Success Factors

**Retention Rate:** 70% freshman retention indicates students are returning for their second year at rates similar to national peers.

**Completion Challenge:** The gap emerges in converting retained students into graduates:
- Strong first-year retention (70%)
- Lower 4-year completion (21%)
- Lower 6-year completion (25%)
- Lower 8-year completion (31%)

## Transfer and Attrition Patterns

**Of non-completing students:**
- Nearly 1 in 4 (23.6%) successfully transfer to other institutions
- More than 2 in 3 (70.3%) drop out without completing or transferring
- Less than 1% remain enrolled beyond 8 years
