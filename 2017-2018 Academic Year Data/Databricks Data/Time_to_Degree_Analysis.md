# 2017-2018 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2017 - June 30, 2018

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 135 | 59.2% |
| 4-5 years | 53 | 23.2% |
| 5-6 years | 16 | 7.0% |
| 6-7 years | 3 | 1.3% |
| 7+ years | 21 | 9.2% |

**Total Analyzed:** 228 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 135 graduates (59.2%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 53 graduates (23.2%)
- **5-6 years:** 16 graduates (7.0%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 69 graduates (30.3%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 3 graduates (1.3%)
- **7+ years:** 21 graduates (9.2%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 24 graduates (10.5%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Majority complete quickly:** 59.2% finish in under 4 years (likely transfer students)
- **Significant extended completion:** 30.3% take 4-6 years
- **Long-term persistence:** 10.5% take 6+ years to complete
