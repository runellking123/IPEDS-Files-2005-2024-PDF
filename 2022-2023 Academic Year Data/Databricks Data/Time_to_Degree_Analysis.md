# 2022-2023 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2022 - June 30, 2023

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 50 | 53.2% |
| 4-5 years | 15 | 16.0% |
| 5-6 years | 12 | 12.8% |
| 6-7 years | 7 | 7.4% |
| 7+ years | 10 | 10.6% |

**Total Analyzed:** 94 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 50 graduates (53.2%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 15 graduates (16.0%)
- **5-6 years:** 12 graduates (12.8%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 27 graduates (28.7%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 7 graduates (7.4%)
- **7+ years:** 10 graduates (10.6%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 17 graduates (18.1%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Majority complete quickly:** 53.2% finish in under 4 years (likely transfer students)
- **Significant extended completion:** 28.7% take 4-6 years
- **Long-term persistence:** 18.1% take 6+ years to complete
