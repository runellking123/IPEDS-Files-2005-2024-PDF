# 2021-2022 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2021 - June 30, 2022

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 49 | 45.4% |
| 4-5 years | 34 | 31.5% |
| 5-6 years | 9 | 8.3% |
| 6-7 years | 5 | 4.6% |
| 7+ years | 11 | 10.2% |

**Total Analyzed:** 108 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 49 graduates (45.4%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 34 graduates (31.5%)
- **5-6 years:** 9 graduates (8.3%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 43 graduates (39.8%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 5 graduates (4.6%)
- **7+ years:** 11 graduates (10.2%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 16 graduates (14.8%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Significant extended completion:** 39.8% take 4-6 years
- **Long-term persistence:** 14.8% take 6+ years to complete
