# 2020-2021 Academic Year - Time-to-Degree Analysis

**Data Source:** Wiley University Databricks (Jenzabar J1 Database)
**Query Date:** December 16, 2025
**Period:** July 1, 2020 - June 30, 2021

---

## Overview

This analysis examines the time required for bachelor's degree completion, measuring from initial entry date to degree conferral date.

---

## Time to Bachelor's Degree Completion

| Time Category | Graduates | Percentage |
|---------------|-----------|------------|
| < 4 years | 89 | 69.5% |
| 4-5 years | 24 | 18.8% |
| 5-6 years | 6 | 4.7% |
| 6-7 years | 2 | 1.6% |
| 7+ years | 7 | 5.5% |

**Total Analyzed:** 128 bachelor's degree recipients with entry date data

---

## Completion Rate Analysis

### Traditional 4-Year Track
- **Under 4 years:** 89 graduates (69.5%)
- Most students in this category are likely transfer students with prior credits

### Extended Completion (4-6 Years)
- **4-5 years:** 24 graduates (18.8%)
- **5-6 years:** 6 graduates (4.7%)
- **Combined 4-6 years:** 30 graduates (23.4%)

### Long-Term Completers (6+ Years)
- **6-7 years:** 2 graduates (1.6%)
- **7+ years:** 7 graduates (5.5%)
- **Combined 6+ years:** 9 graduates (7.0%)

---

## Key Insights

- **Majority complete quickly:** 69.5% finish in under 4 years (likely transfer students)
- **Significant extended completion:** 23.4% take 4-6 years
